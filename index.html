Know your brew

Understanding various coffee brewing methods is like holding the key to a treasure chest. Each method, from the Chemex's clean profiles to the French Press's robust depths, allows you to tailor your coffee to your mood and needs. You can kick-start a sluggish morning with the gentle touch of a V60 or ease into a hot afternoon with the subtle grace of a Cold Brew.

The Methods

V60

V60 enthusiasts are the tinkerers and perfectionists of the coffee world. This method appeals to those who enjoy experimenting with their brew, adjusting variables like grind size, water temperature, and pouring technique to achieve the perfect cup. The V60 offers unparalleled control over the brewing process, making it ideal for coffee drinkers who love to dive deep into the craft and appreciate the bright, nuanced flavors it can extract from single-origin beans.

Learn more

Chemex

Chemex is for the purist at heart. Its unique design and brewing technique produce a coffee that is clean, pure and free of bitterness, emphasizing the subtle nuances of the coffee bean. If you delight in the clarity and complexity of flavors and value a method that doubles as a centerpiece, the Chemex is your perfect match. Ideal for light to medium roast beans.

Learn more

French Press

This is the method for those who crave a rich, robust cup of coffee. It's perfect if you prefer your brew full-bodied and full of flavor, with all the natural oils and sediments included. This method is less about finesse and more about simplicity and strength. It's particularly suited to darker roasts and those chilly mornings when only the richest coffee will do.

Learn more

Cold Brew

Cold Brew is the method for the patient and the planner, those who appreciate the smoother, sweeter side of coffee without the acidity often found in its hot-brewed counterparts. This method is ideal for coffee lovers who seek a refreshing, mellow cup that can be enjoyed over hours or even days. Perfect if you enjoy a rich, concentrated flavor over ice on a warm day. It's especially suited for those who prefer a less bitter, more approachable coffee experience, offering a taste profile that is both bold and smooth.

Learning the Basics

Grind Size and Extraction

Adjust According to Method: Finer grinds for quick extraction methods; coarser grinds for slower methods like French press.

Consistency is Crucial: A uniform grind promotes even extraction and optimal flavor.

Water Temperature Wisdom

Ideal Range: Maintain water temperature between 90°C to 96°C for most brewing methods to avoid bitterness.

Pre-Warm Equipment: Ensuring your brewing equipment is pre-warmed can help maintain the optimal temperature throughout the brewing process.

Coffee to Water Ratio

Start with a Standard: Begin with a general ratio of 1:16 (coffee to water) and adjust to taste for strength and body.

Precision Weighing: Use a scale for both coffee and water to achieve consistent results.

Mastering Pour-Over

Precision is Key: Achieve a clean and nuanced cup by controlling the flow and temperature of water. A gooseneck kettle enhances precision, ensuring an even saturation of grounds.

Grind Size Matters: A medium-fine grind works best, allowing for optimal extraction without over-extraction.

French Press Fundamentals

Coarse Grind for Richness: Use a coarse grind to prevent over-extraction and ensure a full-bodied flavor.

Steeping Perfection: Allow the coffee to steep for four minutes before pressing, balancing strength and clarity.

Cold Brew Basics

Patience Pays Off: Steep coarse grounds in cold water for 12 to 24 hours for a smooth and mellow flavor profile.

Ratio and Time: Experiment with the coffee-to-water ratio and steeping time to find your ideal strength and taste.
